# CreteKos Marketing Strategy

## Brand Positioning

**One line:** Single-estate Cretan olive oil for India's most intentional kitchens.

**The wedge:** India imports ~₹3,500 Cr of olive oil annually, but 90%+ is blended, Italian-branded commodity oil. CreteKos is the opposite — single grove, named harvest, traceable to the tree. It enters the market not as "olive oil for India" but as a *provenance story* that happens to be olive oil.

**Target persona:** Urban Indian, 28–45, household income ₹15L+, already cooking with olive oil (or ghee-forward and curious). Thinks about food origin. Shops at Nature's Basket, Foodhall, or online. Follows food creators, not fitness influencers.

---

## Phase 1: Founding 400 (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** Sell the first 400 bottles to people who will *talk about it*.

### 1. Founding Bottle Drop (scarcity + story)
- 400 hand-numbered bottles, not 2,400. Create urgency.
- Waitlist on site → invite-only purchase link.
- Each bottle ships with the bilingual recipe card + a handwritten harvest note.
- Price: ₹2,400. No discounts. Ever.

### 2. Chef Seeding (10 chefs, 10 cities)
- Send 2 bottles + recipe card to 10 chefs who cook Indian food with global ingredients:
  - **Mumbai:** Amninder Sandhu (Arth), Hussain Shahzad (O Pedro)
  - **Delhi:** Prateek Sadhu (Masque alum), Megha Kohli
  - **Bengaluru:** Manu Chandra (Toast & Tonic), Farmlore team
  - **Goa, Chennai, Kolkata:** 1 each
- Ask for nothing. No #ad. Just send the oil with a note: *"We'd love to know what you make with it."*
- Follow up 2 weeks later. Repost anything they share.

### 3. WhatsApp-First Community
- "Letters from Chania" WhatsApp broadcast (not group).
- Monthly: harvest update, one recipe, one photo from the grove.
- This becomes the highest-converting channel. Olive oil is a considered purchase — WhatsApp is where trust lives in India.

### 4. Instagram: Editorial, Not Promotional
- **Content pillars:**
  - **The Grove** — photos/videos from Chania, harvest, press day
  - **The Indian Kitchen** — chefs and home cooks finishing dishes with the oil
  - **Two Tables** — side-by-side: a Cretan dish and its Indian parallel
  - **The Bottle** — unboxing, wax seal, hand-numbering
- **Tone:** Quiet confidence. No "Buy now!" No reels with trending audio. Think *Kinfolk* meets *Vogue India Food*.
- **Post frequency:** 4–5x/week. Stories daily during harvest.

## Phase 2: First 2,400 (Months 4–8)

**Goal:** Sell out Harvest 2025. Build repeat purchase.

### 5. Content Engine: Blog / Learn Section
- Publish 2 articles/month:
  - "The Perfect Tadka with EVOO" → SEO for "olive oil tadka"
  - "5 Indian Dishes Better with EVOO" → SEO for "olive oil Indian cooking"
  - "Polyphenols in Indian Diets" → SEO for "olive oil health benefits India"
- Each article ends with a soft CTA to the product page. No pop-ups.

### 6. Subscription / Refill Model
- **"The Quarterly Bottle"** — auto-ship every 3 months, ₹2,200/bottle (₹200 off).
- Frame it as a harvest membership, not a subscription box.
- Members get the new harvest 2 weeks before anyone else.

### 7. Gifting Play (Diwali / Christmas)
- ₹2,400 olive oil in a wax-sealed box is a *perfect* premium gift.
- **"Two Tables" gift set:** 1 bottle + recipe card + a small bag of Cretan oregano + Kashmiri saffron (₹3,200).
- Target corporate gifting: 50-bottle minimums with custom numbering.
- **Timeline:** Pre-Diwali launch (August) for October delivery.

### 8. Foodhall / Nature's Basket Pilot
- 2–3 flagship stores only. Not wide distribution.
- In-store tasting station: bread, oil, salt. That's it.
- QR code on the shelf talker → grove video.
- Scarcity messaging: *"Harvest 2025 — limited bottles remaining."*

---

## Phase 3: Brand Building (Months 9–18)

### 9. "Two Tables" Dinner Series
- Quarterly pop-up dinners: one Greek course, one Indian course, same oil.
- Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru. 30 seats, ₹3,500/head.
- Co-host with a local chef. Film it. Use for content.

### 10. India ↔ Crete Exchange
- Fly 2 Indian food creators to Chania for harvest season (November).
- They document the grove, the press, the family. Authentic content, not influencer tourism.
- Reverse: bring Antonis/Stelios to Mumbai for a week of cooking with Indian chefs.

### 11. PR: Food + Lifestyle, Not "Startup"
- **Target:** Condé Nast Traveller India, Vogue India (food), The Hindu Weekend, Mint Lounge.
- **Story angle:** *"The Indian founders who went to Crete and came back with olive oil."*
- **Avoid:** "Disruptive" startup language. This is a grove, not a unicorn.

## The One Rule

**Never discount. Never do flash sales. Never say "limited time offer."**

The scarcity is real — there are only 2,400 bottles. The price is the price. The brand is built on the idea that good oil, like good food, doesn't need to shout. Let the grove, the oil, and the people who cook with it do the talking.
